This evening, we will use our innate connection to the realm of spirits to channel the one that haunts these halls. We shall learn of the message which it wishes to impart to the land of the living, and we shall a"empt to piece together its ghastly tale so that it may #nd peace.

Prepare yourselves in mind, body, and most importantly, spirit.

Let us now begin.

How To Play

In Séance, players take on the role of mid-19th century Mediums attempting to communicate with a troublesome Spirit.

This is a co-operative role-playing game. That means you should speak and act as your character would. You’ll also get a chance to play the role of the Spirit.

The ultimate goal is to reveal each Medium’s Message and piece together the story of the Spirit.

What you’ll need:

  1. At least two willing participants.
  2. Writing implements.
  3. Paper.
  4. A 5 minute timer.

To get started:

  1. Read the rules.
  2. Set the mood if you’d like. Candles, crystal balls, and appropriate music can all help players get in to their roles.
  3. Write down your Medium’s name, appearance, 10 Channeling Points, and a Message. Extra details are welcome, but not mechanically significant.
  4. Read the Introduction out loud.
  5. Introduce your Mediums.
  6. Whenever you’re ready to begin in earnest, the player who most recently experienced something unexplainable begins Channeling !e Spirit.

Rules

Your Character. This game takes place in the mid 1800s. Pick an appropriate name and persona. That’s your Medium, and you act as them unless you’re Channeling The Spirit.

The Message. While writing down your Medium’s details, you should secretly write down one word on a separate piece of paper. "is word is your Message. It should be speci#c. Avoid common words that occur frequently in casual conversation. Your goal is to get other Mediums to guess your Message while you’re Channeling "e Spirit. You can never say your Message out loud, write it down (aside from during character creation), spell it out, or otherwise give it away. Instead, you must provide hints and clues. If another Medium correctly guesses your Message, immediately reveal your Message and place it in the middle of the table.

Channeling !e Spirit. To Channel !e Spirit, deduct 1 Channeling Point from your Medium. Whomever is Channeling "e Spirit resumes the role of their Medium and you begin speaking and acting as the Spirit instead.

When a Medium #rst starts Channeling, start a 5 minute timer. All other Mediums should engage them to learn more about the Spirit. A$er 5 minutes have passed, other Mediums may spend Channeling Points to take over if they wish.

While Channeling, you should either use mannerisms, voices, accents, and behaviors previously established by other Mediums, or introduce new ones. You may shape the Spirit’s personality and traits as you wish, as long as you never contradict a trait already established by another Medium. "e Spirit will become more concrete as each Medium has a chance to shape them by Channeling.
